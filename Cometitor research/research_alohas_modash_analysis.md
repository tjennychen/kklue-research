## Program Scale

- 1,021 unique creators, 1,590 posts in collaborators.json (no API cap hit -- API returned exactly what was fetched)
- Date range: Aug 2020 to Mar 2026. The real program starts in 2024. Pre-2024 is sparse (under 100 posts total for 3+ years).
- 2024-2025 combined: 1,388 posts. That's the actual operating cadence.
- Avg 1.56 posts per creator. Most are one-shot. Program is wide, not deep.
- Program accelerated sharply in 2025: 618 posts vs 455 in 2024. Growing fast.

## Special Programs: The Footwear Content Layer

Alohas is a shoe brand. 39.4% of all posts (627) include explicit footwear terms (ballet flat, boot, sandal, loafer, etc.) and those posts generate 44.2% of total likes. That's proportional, not outsized -- shoe content isn't disproportionately viral, it just tracks with volume.

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Posts with footwear keywords | 627 (39.4%) |
| Total likes from footwear posts | 1,828,619 (44.2%) |
| Fashion week tagged posts | 12 (0.8%) |
| Sustainability-tagged posts | 72 (4.5%) |

**Notable:** @bubusimplisticlife's 198K-like affiliate post was a shoelace life-hack video -- not a style post. Utility content (how to tie shoes) outperformed aesthetic content. Fashion week posts are minimal (12 posts, 0.8%) -- not a strategy play. Sustainability is a brand pillar but creators barely use it (4.5%).

The program skews heavily European/UK (154 posts with major EU city tags, 47 UK-tagged posts) with a strong Spanish-language cluster (112 posts, 11.7% of all likes). Alohas is Spanish, and their home market audience still drives meaningful engagement.

## Whitespace for KKlue

**Different categories, shared creator pool.** Alohas is shoes. KKlue is jewelry. They both live in "accessible luxury DTC accessories." The fashion/style micro-creator who posts Alohas ballet flats is the exact same creator type who would post fine gold jewelry from KKlue. This isn't competition -- it's a shared audience.

**Asian and Korean creators are a real but underexploited pocket.** @nizinanjjang (Korean, 9 posts, 10K likes) and @wenyiteong (Malaysian, 9 posts) are in Alohas' program. @charlizeechiu (16K likes, single post), @theheychu (Chinese-language, 4K likes), @itsjulialee (2K likes). That's 5-6 Asian creators in the entire 1,021-creator dataset. Alohas hasn't built here intentionally. KKlue's Korean-American identity is a genuine differentiator in this space -- it's an untouched lane.

**The affiliate model is scalable and Alohas proved it.** 223 affiliate posts, 86 using LTK/ShopMy tooling. The infrastructure is proven in this creator category. KKlue should stand up affiliate infrastructure (ShopMy or Shopify Collabs) before recruiting -- it signals professionalism and gives creators a reason to post beyond gifting.

**European program saturation.** Alohas' program is deeply European: UK, Spain, France, Italy. That market is already served. KKlue's US-first positioning + diaspora angle has less direct competition from Alohas' creator base.

**Disclosure arbitrage.** 83.5% of Alohas posts are undisclosed or vague. If KKlue enforces proper disclosure as a standard, it's a brand trust signal -- especially relevant as FTC enforcement picks up.

## Skeptic's Core Point

Strip out the top 5 posts (1.1M likes, 27.5% of all likes from 5 posts out of 1,590) and the remaining 1,585 posts average 1,895 likes each. That sounds decent, but the median is 240 likes. The program is not 1,895 likes/post -- it's 240 likes/post with fat tails from a few Indian and Spanish viral creators who probably would have posted organically anyway. @officialvidhiyadav's 309K-like post lists Alohas as one of five brands in the caption. That's not an ambassador relationship. It's a lucky tag. Alohas has scale, not depth.
