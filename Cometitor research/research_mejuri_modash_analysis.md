**Date:** 2026-03-29
**Source:** mejuri-top100.json (100 raw entries), mejuri-midtier-100-1000likes.json (641 entries), mejuri-collaborators.json (2000 posts, API cap)
**After deduplication:** 1,966 unique posts from 1,100 unique creators

## Program Scale

- 1,100 unique creators. 2,000 posts captured (API cap -- actual program is larger).
- Date range: May 2024 to March 2026. Nearly 2 full years of data.
- Avg posts per creator: 1.82. But that average is misleading.
- 764 creators posted exactly once (69.5%). 336 creators posted 2 or more times.
- The program looks like a mix: a high-churn gifting/paid layer plus a smaller repeat-poster core.
- 3.544M total likes across 2,000 posts. That sounds big. The concentration tells a different story (see below).

## Whitespace for KKlue

**Asian-American representation is thin but present.** Roughly 18 creators in this dataset have visible Asian/South Asian identity signals (handles, descriptions, aesthetics). They account for ~144 posts and 120K likes -- about 3.4% of total likes from 1.1% of the creator pool. That's actually above-average engagement density, but Mejuri has clearly not made Asian-American audiences a strategic focus. @mimixn (18 posts, 31K likes) and @byalicesigh (12 posts, 12K likes) are the most activated. The niche is lightly touched, not owned.

**Mejuri's Korean/Asian jewelry aesthetic gap.** Mejuri positions as "fine jewelry for every day" with a Canadian minimalist identity. That's a different brand register than KKlue's Korean-American design aesthetic. Creators in Asian-American fashion and K-beauty adjacent niches have visible Mejuri posts but no concentrated program aimed at them. KKlue can own this niche by default.

**The paid tier is the weakest.** 915 paid posts, median 530 likes, avg 1,274 likes. At scale, paid posts are underperforming affiliate (median 596, avg 2,537) and unspecified (avg 2,752). A program that uses affiliate-first structure instead of paid-first would likely generate better engagement per dollar. KKlue can start affiliate-only and skip the low-performing paid tier entirely.

**Gifting is underpowered at Mejuri's scale.** 279 gifted posts, median 284 likes. The gifting program exists but generates the weakest engagement per post. For a smaller brand, gifting done more selectively (10-20 well-matched creators vs. Mejuri's spray approach) would show better ROI.

**Evergreen is possible and Mejuri proves it.** Mejuri runs 75-130 posts a month year-round. That's 900-1,500 posts/year. KKlue doesn't need to match that -- but the template exists for a 24/7 program rather than gorjana's holiday-only approach. 20-30 consistently active creators, refreshed quarterly, would create that cadence.

**No clear nail art or tattoo adjacent niche.** Mejuri has 29 creators mentioning nails in their content (incidental product mentions, not partnerships). Zero tattoo content. Fine jewelry in tattoo culture is a real aesthetic overlap. KKlue could activate that niche completely uncontested.

## Skeptic's Core Point

Mejuri's 3.5M likes across 2,000 posts sounds like an enormous program. Strip out the top 10 creators and you lose 53% of all likes. The program is actually propped up by @maybeboth (an LTK lifestyle creator), one Bollywood celebrity post, a WTA tennis ambassador, and a comedy/dating content creator. The remaining 1,090+ creators generate under half the likes. Paid partnerships -- the largest bucket at 46% of posts -- have the lowest median engagement. At this scale, Mejuri is running a broadcast program, not a community program. KKlue doesn't need to compete with that. A focused 20-30 creator program in Asian-American fashion and lifestyle niches, structured around affiliate from day one, would likely outperform Mejuri's entire mid-tier per dollar spent.
