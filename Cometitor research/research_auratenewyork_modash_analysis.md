# Aurate New York Influencer Strategy: Modash Data Analysis
**Date:** 2026-03-29
**Source:** auratenewyork-top100.json (100 raw entries), auratenewyork-midtier-100-1000likes.json (492 entries)
**After deduplication:** 953 unique posts from 664 unique creators
**Framework:** research_influencer_dtc_best_practices.md

---

## Program Scale

- 664 unique creators, 953 unique posts across ~8 years (Oct 2017 - Feb 2026)
- Avg 1.44 posts per creator. Most relationships are one-shot.
- 174 creators posted 2+ times. Only a small core has any ongoing relationship.
- This is a volume program, not a depth program.

## Disclosure Compliance

- **Properly disclosed: 573 posts (60.1%)**
- **Likely hidden sponsorship: 374 posts (39.2%)**
- **Other/unclear: 6 posts (0.6%)**

40% of their program runs undisclosed. That's still an FTC risk, but notably better than some competitors. The "Likely Hidden" bucket is heavy in Affiliate and Unspecified posts -- comment-gating links, ShopMy drop-ins, multi-brand tag posts where Aurate is one of several.

Most of the "Paid" type is properly disclosed (559 of 565 paid posts carry a proper disclosure label). Their direct brand deals appear clean. The mess is in the affiliate/unspecified layer.

Every high-CLR creator here is running comment-gating ("comment NECKLACE for link") or a giveaway entry mechanic. The comments are bots and entries, not real conversations. @rachlondon at 225.6% CLR has 1K likes and 2.3K comments -- that's a giveaway entry post asking followers to tag friends.

Aurate has no visible "high authentic CLR" creators in this dataset. The organic conversation isn't happening at scale.

## Special Program: Giveaway as Activation Tactic

Aurate runs giveaways as a recurring influencer tactic -- not a standalone program, but embedded throughout creator partnerships.

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Giveaway posts | 142 (14.9% of total) |
| Unique giveaway creators | 125 |
| Total giveaway likes (all) | 312,670 (19.2% of all likes) |
| Total giveaway likes (ex-KW) | 126,859 (19.0% of non-KW likes) |
| Median giveaway likes | 342 |

Giveaway posts punch above median (342 vs 199 overall), but that's the comment inflation effect. Real engagement is lower than it looks.

Notable: Kerry Washington's giveaway posts are by far the highest performers. @faithcollins ran a Christmas giveaway for 25.8K likes. @xomacenna ran a Rose Garden Collection giveaway for 7.9K. Remove Kerry and giveaways produce 127K likes across 129 posts -- about 984 likes per post on average. That's respectable but not exceptional.

Giveaway is a volume acquisition tactic. It inflates follower counts for the creator, not brand equity for Aurate.

## Whitespace for KKlue

**Celebrity anchor is their whole game.** Kerry Washington is 59% of Aurate's total likes across all posts. KKlue cannot play in that lane at launch. Don't try.

**Asian/Korean creator presence = nearly zero.** Three posts with any Asian cultural signal in the entire 953-post dataset: @iamkouka (Japanese cooking + medicine niche), @thesunkissedblasian (giveaway), @itshuyennguyen (New Year code post). None are Korean-American lifestyle or fashion creators. None are doing Korean aesthetic content. This is the biggest gap in their program -- and it overlaps directly with KKlue's natural audience.

**Giveaway tactic is overused and inflated.** 125 different creators ran giveaways for Aurate. The mechanic is played out. KKlue should avoid giveaways as a core tactic -- audiences are fatigued and the CLR numbers don't reflect real community. Build with creators who have organic jewelry love, not contest farmers.

**Affiliate layer is bloated and low-quality.** 195 affiliate posts, 103 with zero likes. The affiliate program has scale but zero curation. KKlue can own a tight, high-quality affiliate program (ShopMy or similar) with 10-20 genuinely aligned micro-influencers and outperform Aurate's affiliate layer on a per-creator basis.

**No evergreen ambassador relationships outside KW.** @xomacenna's Rose Garden Collection (co-designed jewelry line) is the only creator collab that looks like a real partnership. Everything else is transactional. KKlue has the opportunity to build 3-5 deep creator relationships with Korean-American and Asian-American fashion/lifestyle creators who are invested in the brand long-term.

**Spring activation window is uncrowded.** Apr-Jun is their heaviest posting period. Valentine's Day and Mother's Day are natural peaks for fine jewelry. If KKlue launches with micro-creator content in those windows, there's no "gorjana owns this season" problem to fight against.

## Skeptic's Core Point

This is a Kerry Washington vanity program. Remove her 13 posts and Aurate's 940 remaining posts produce 666K total likes -- a median of 192 likes per post. 74% of posts get under 500 likes. The affiliate layer has 103 posts with zero engagement. What looks like a 953-post influencer powerhouse is, at median, a mid-tier gifting operation propped up by one celebrity deal that mostly ran in 2020-2021. KKlue doesn't need to beat that. A focused program of 20-30 Korean-American and Asian-American micro-influencers with genuine alignment would likely outperform Aurate's non-KW median on less budget.
