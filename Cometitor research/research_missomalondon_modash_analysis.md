**Date:** 2026-03-29
**Source:** missomalondon-top100.json (100 raw entries), missomalondon-midtier-100-1000likes.json (379 entries)
**After deduplication:** 1,094 unique posts from 581 unique creators
**Framework:** research_influencer_dtc_best_practices.md

---

## Program Scale

- 581 unique creators, 1,094 total posts spanning 9 years (May 2017 to March 2026)
- Program has scaled dramatically: 2024 saw ~330 posts, 2025 saw ~468 posts
- Avg posts per creator: 1.9 -- but 168 creators have posted more than once
- This is a large, mature program with deep UK roots and a handful of signature named collabs

| Affiliate | 244 | 22.3% | 325 | 1,888 | 460,695 |
| Ambassador | 10 | 0.9% | 280 | 1,150 | 11,504 |

**Key read:** Paid is the backbone (29.4% of posts, best median at 910 likes). Gifted has a bloated average (5,247) entirely driven by one viral outlier (@cherrychy_ with 942K likes on a gifted post). Strip that out and gifted median is 168 -- worst of any type.

**Surprising find:** The affiliate program is bigger than gifted in post count and has meaningfully better median engagement (325 vs 168). Missoma has clearly invested in building an LTK/affiliate layer that's performing better per post than pure gifting.

**Shift happening:** In 2025, affiliate became the dominant format (36.1% of recent posts), with paid shrinking to 11.5%. This is a deliberate pivot toward affiliate-first.

## Disclosure Compliance

- **Properly disclosed: 409 posts (37.4%)**
- **Likely hidden sponsorship: 665 posts (60.8%)**
- **Unclear: 20 posts (1.8%)**

60% of their program is running undisclosed. Notably better than average (gorjana was at 80% hidden), but still a significant FTC risk. Most undisclosed posts are affiliate links (LTK mentions, promo codes, "comment SHOP") -- technically a gray area but Modash flags them as likely hidden.

## Special Programs

**Named collabs are the real engine.** Missoma runs ongoing co-design partnerships, not just one-off gifting:

- **Lucy Williams x Missoma** (@lucywilliams02): 45 posts across 3+ years (2022-2025). 166,841 total likes. Active affiliate with personal promo code (LUCY30). Longest running named collab in the dataset.
- **Harris Reed x Missoma** (@harris_reed + @fluidbyharrisreed): 11 posts, 94,024 total likes. Gender-fluid designer collab, launched 2022. Black Friday activation in 2024-2025.
- **Savi x Missoma** (@savislook): 11 posts, Chinese fashion creator based in Beijing. Three full collections (2022 Rabbit Year, 2024 Snake Year, plus core line). Posts in Mandarin. This is Missoma's explicit China market play.

**UK creator concentration is dominant.** Of 1,094 posts, ~716 (65%) have UK-specific signals (hashtags, British brands tagged, language). US creator signals appear in just 14 posts.

**The few US/global breakout posts are huge.** @cherrychy_ (US creator, 942K likes gifted), @oliviakmorris (132K, unspecified), @sukiwaterhouse (105K paid), @nathalieemmanuel (105K unspecified). All US or US-audience creators, all high-reach, all relatively one-off. There's no systematic US creator program -- these are exceptions.

**Celebrity and fashion collab tier:** @tamara (influencer/model, 8 posts, 239K likes), @lydiajanetomlinson (13 posts, 111K likes), @sophiemoulds (4 posts, 97K likes). This tier is UK fashion-media adjacent.

**Top 5 creators generate 43.3% of all likes. Top 10 generate 55.9%.** The program is extremely top-heavy.

## Whitespace for KKlue

**The US creator base is nearly empty.** 65%+ of Missoma's posts are UK-centric. Only 14 posts have US signals. Their US breakout posts (cherrychy_, sukiwaterhouse, nathalieemmanuel, oliviakmorris) are all one-offs, never converted into ongoing relationships. Missoma does not have a US influencer program. This is the entire whitespace.

**No Asian-American creator presence.** The Savi collab targets mainland Chinese consumers on Chinese social platforms -- it's a separate play for a different market. Zero posts in this dataset from Asian-American creators (US diaspora, second-gen, etc.). Not one. This is KKlue's clearest lane: Korean-American and Asian-American lifestyle, fashion, and jewelry creators. Missoma has not touched this audience in the US at all.

**The affiliate model is being built right now.** Missoma's recent shift (2025: 36% affiliate posts vs only 12% paid) shows they're investing in LTK-style affiliate. But almost all those affiliate creators are UK-based. An early US affiliate buildout on LTK and ShopMy, targeting Asian-American micro creators, has zero direct competition from Missoma.

**Paid is expensive and thin in the US.** Missoma's paid US posts are celebrity-tier (Suki Waterhouse, Nathalie Emmanuel). These are aspirational brand moments, not conversion drivers. KKlue can't compete on celebrity paid spend -- but can dominate the 10K-100K follower tier that Missoma hasn't built in the US.

**Named collab format is worth studying.** Lucy Williams x Missoma ran for 3+ years, 45 posts, personal promo codes, Black Friday activations. Harris Reed x Missoma targets a non-binary/fashion-forward audience. The co-design collab is core to Missoma's identity. KKlue could run a Korean-American designer collab or a Korean diaspora cultural collab as a signature program rather than generic gifting.

**Disclosure is mediocre.** 60% of Missoma posts are undisclosed. If KKlue launches with clean FTC compliance and signals this intentionally to press and buyers, it's a genuine differentiator in a category where everyone is cutting corners.

## Skeptic's Core Point

Missoma's program looks massive (1,094 posts, 581 creators) but the median post gets 340 likes. @cherrychy_'s single 942K-like gifted post is almost entirely responsible for the inflated gifted average. The actual gifted median is 168 likes. The program's real workhorse is @lucywilliams02 -- a 3-year named affiliate collab with consistent mid-five-figure engagement. Strip that out and the ambassador/named collab tier, and what remains is a high-volume UK micro-gifting program performing at low double-digit median likes. KKlue should not try to replicate this at scale. Building 3-5 deep named affiliate relationships in the US Asian-American fashion space would likely outperform Missoma's entire US presence on a per-dollar basis.
