**Date:** 2026-03-29
**Source:** adinaeden_-top100.json (100 raw entries), adinaeden_-midtier-100-1000likes.json (90 entries)
**After deduplication:** 143 unique posts from 106 unique creators (brand-owned accounts excluded: @adinaeden_bk, @itsadinaeden)
**Framework:** research_influencer_dtc_best_practices.md

---

## Program Scale

- 106 unique creators, 143 unique posts across 8 years (May 2018 - Mar 2026)
- The 2018 data is a statistical anomaly: two Brazilian macro influencers (@anchieta, @silvialicius) with 100K+ likes each. Ignore for program benchmarking.
- Strip pre-2020 data: 100 recent creators, 140 posts, ~18 months of meaningful signal
- Average 1.35 posts per creator. This program is mostly one-and-done transactional relationships.
- 28 creators posted more than once. Only 3 posted 3+ times.

**Key read:** Paid posts generate the most total likes (245K) but the average is inflated by a handful of macro hits. The median paid post only gets 205 likes. Unspecified posts (hidden sponsorships) carry enormous average likes (4,957) driven by the viral outliers. Strip those outliers and the picture is grim across all types.

**Affiliate is volume, not performance.** 42 affiliate posts, median 121 likes. This is a high-density, low-engagement program. Lots of LTK/ShopMy links, very little organic pull.

## Disclosure Compliance

- **Properly disclosed: 43 posts (30.1%)**
- **Likely hidden sponsorship: 88 posts (61.5%)**
- **Unclear: 12 posts (8.4%)**

60%+ of posts are undisclosed. This is an FTC risk. Most of those hidden posts are affiliate links dressed up as organic recommendations. Common mechanics in the data: "comment 'link' and I'll DM you," ShopLTK links buried in captions, promo codes with no #ad or #gifted.

In the 2024+ era, affiliate has become Adina Eden's dominant channel. 42% of all recent posts are affiliate. Almost none of them disclose properly.

## Whitespace for KKlue

**Affiliate density is their core strategy now.** 42% of 2024+ posts are affiliate. Almost none disclose. KKlue can undercut this with a clean, properly disclosed affiliate program. Shopify Collabs or ShopMy with mandatory #gifted/#aff disclosure builds trust with both creators and regulators.

**Asian/Korean creators are absent.** One post from @priscillamoy with 307 likes. That's it. Zero Korean-American, Japanese-American, or pan-Asian aesthetic creators in Adina Eden's entire program. KKlue has a genuine differentiation play here: the Korean fine jewelry aesthetic, kcom/kstyle lifestyle adjacent, and Asian-American diaspora fashion. No competitor has claimed this lane.

**The Jewish identity niche is real but small.** Adina Eden should own this niche given the brand's identity, but barely two posts reflect it. Not a lane KKlue needs to worry about. If anything, it signals Adina Eden isn't executing on their own brand story.

**No lifestyle verticals beyond fashion.** The entire program is fashion/outfit content. No home, no beauty, no food, no travel (beyond one Italy post). KKlue can differentiate with broader lifestyle embedding: "wear KKlue to your family dinner," "stack KKlue for your work trip to Seoul," etc.

**The viral outliers are not repeatable.** @mrunu (88K likes, piercing video) and two 2018 Brazilian macros are statistical noise. Adina Eden can't replicate these. KKlue shouldn't chase viral. Build compounding micro.

---

## Skeptic's Core Point

Strip the two 2018 Brazilian macro influencer posts and one viral piercing video from 2024, and this program's median is 139 likes per post. 70% of posts stay under 500 likes. The affiliate layer is enormous (42% of recent posts) and almost entirely undisclosed. This isn't a sophisticated influencer program. It's a loose affiliate network that generates modest conversion volume but no meaningful brand lift. KKlue doesn't need to beat this. A tighter program of 20-30 properly disclosed Asian-American micro-creators with genuine audiences would out-perform Adina Eden's median on brand perception and likely on CLR too.
