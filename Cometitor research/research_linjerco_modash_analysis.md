## Program Scale

- 508 unique creators, 948 unique posts spanning 2018-2026 (earliest: Aug 2018, latest: Mar 2026)
- Avg 1.87 posts per creator. Mostly one-shot relationships, but a meaningful long-tail of repeat posters
- Program has scaled dramatically in 2024-2025. The first five years were sparse; 2024-2025 alone account for the majority of recent volume
- This is a big program. Much bigger than gorjana's 90-post dataset. More noise, but also a real affiliate engine

**Surprising find:** Unspecified posts (undisclosed) outperform paid on both median (497 vs 188) and avg (680 vs 354). Their hidden mentions outperform their official paid partnerships.

## Disclosure Compliance

Breakdown by Modash label (more accurate than type field alone):

- **Properly disclosed: 437 posts (46.1%)**
- **Likely hidden sponsorship: 509 posts (53.7%)**
- **Unclear: 2 posts (0.2%)**

The disclosure picture is messier than it looks. 87.7% of affiliate posts are labeled "Likely Hidden Sponsorship" -- these creators use promo codes and @tags but don't add #ad. Only 54 of 439 affiliate posts have proper disclosure.

**Engagement from hidden posts: 368,696 likes (72.3% of all likes)**

More than two-thirds of their total engagement comes from posts that don't disclose. Their best content is undisclosed. FTC risk that scales with their program size.

## Whitespace for KKlue

**They already own the minimalist jewelry affiliate lane.** 238 unique affiliate creators, 46% of all posts. Competing directly with Linjer on affiliate recruitment in the generic lifestyle/fashion space is a losing game.

**The HK/Chinese creator cluster exists, but it's not a KKlue lane.** Linjer has @on_se_voit_a_stutzheim, @kayan.c, @jlouofficial, @ghlchan -- this is a Cantonese-speaking, HK-based audience, not Korean-American. Different cultural identity, different aesthetics. KKlue's lane is Korean-American diaspora and second-gen Asian-American creators in the US. Not a single creator in Linjer's dataset reads as Korean-American.

**Korean-American aesthetic = zero Linjer presence.** KKlue's natural territory: Korean-American fashion, K-beauty adjacent jewelry, second-gen professional women in the US. None of these show up in Linjer's 508-creator roster. This is open ground.

**Paid program is underperforming.** Linjer runs 340 paid posts with median 188 likes. If KKlue builds paid partnerships with tighter creator selection (fewer, higher-quality), they can outperform Linjer's paid tier on efficiency even at smaller scale.

**Evergreen is table stakes.** Linjer runs year-round. Seasonal-only programs (gorjana) leave January-September on the table. KKlue needs an evergreen micro program from day one, not just holiday pushes.

**Ethical/sustainable positioning is already Linjer's.** Recycled gold, lab-grown diamonds, Norwegian minimalism -- that's Linjer's identity. KKlue shouldn't compete on sustainability messaging. The differentiator is cultural identity: Korean craftsmanship, diaspora design sensibility, Asian-American professional women.

## Skeptic's Core Point

Strip out @softgirlari (75K likes, one-hit Valentine's post from 2024) and @the_lifestyle_cog (42K likes, one post from May 2025). Remove those two outliers and Linjer's program median drops to 299 likes per post across 946 remaining posts. They run a massive volume affiliate machine with a long tail of small creators generating forgettable content. The HK cluster is their most interesting asset but it's barely 5% of posts. KKlue doesn't need to match 508 creators. A focused 30-50 creator program hitting Korean-American professional women in the US would occupy a lane Linjer hasn't touched, and likely convert better per dollar given the cultural fit.
