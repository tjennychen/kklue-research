## Material Cost Methodology

Material costs are estimated based on:
- **Gold spot price ~$3,900/troy oz ($125/gram) as of 2025**
- 14K gold = 58.3% purity → ~$73/gram raw material value
- 18K gold = 75% purity → ~$94/gram raw material value
- **Gold vermeil** = 2.5-micron gold layer over sterling silver → minimal gold cost ($0.50–2), mostly silver base ($1–5)
- Typical piece weights: delicate necklace 1–3g, huggies 1–2g, tennis bracelet 5–15g
- Industry markup: **200–300%** over COGS; COGS typically 25–40% of retail

## Key Takeaways for KKlue

- **Sweetspot to target:** $120–$200 AOV (Mejuri/Missoma/Linjer territory) — high volume, accessible self-gifting
- **K-gold (KKlue's material)** sits between vermeil and solid 14K — materially stronger than vermeil, more affordable than solid gold. That's a real differentiator not yet owned in the US market
- **Biggest gap:** No US brand is combining Asian cultural identity + accessible K-gold + personalization at the $100–300 price point
- **Mejuri is the ceiling:** ~$104M in US revenue proves the DTC fine jewelry model works at scale
- **Gorjana is the US-native proof:** $55–85M without a strong cultural identity story — KKlue has a stronger identity angle
