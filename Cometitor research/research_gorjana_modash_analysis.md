# Gorjana Influencer Strategy: Modash Data Analysis
**Date:** 2026-03-29
**Source:** gorjana-top100.json (181 raw entries), gorjana-midtier-100-1000likes.json (71 entries)
**After deduplication:** 90 unique posts from 75 unique creators
**Framework:** research_influencer_dtc_best_practices.md

---

## Program Scale

- 75 unique creators, 90 unique posts across ~6 months (Sep 2025 - Feb 2026)
- That's roughly 1.2 posts per creator. Most relationships are one-shot, not ongoing.
- Only 9 creators posted more than once (see repeat poster table below)

## Partnership Type Breakdown

| Type | Posts | % | Median Likes | Avg Likes | Total Likes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Unspecified | 28 | 31.1% | 438 | 3,694 | 103,427 |
| Gifted | 19 | 21.1% | 643 | 1,534 | 29,138 |
| Affiliate | 17 | 18.9% | 254 | 1,423 | 24,190 |
| Paid | 17 | 18.9% | 370 | 1,802 | 30,628 |
| Ambassador | 9 | 10.0% | 467 | 701 | 6,310 |

**Key read:** "Unspecified" (undisclosed organic/gifted mentions) generates more total likes (103K) than paid (30K), gifted (29K), and affiliate (24K) combined. Their best-performing content doesn't disclose. The audience is responding to what looks organic.

**Surprising find:** Gifted posts have higher median likes (643) than paid posts (370). Paid partnerships aren't generating better engagement than gifted ones.

## Disclosure Compliance

- **Properly disclosed: 18 posts (20.0%)**
- **Likely hidden sponsorship: 70 posts (77.8%)**
- **Unclear: 2 posts (2.2%)**

80% of their influencer program is running undisclosed. This is an FTC risk. It also means most of their "engagement" is coming from posts that audiences don't perceive as ads.

## The Athlete Program: Disproportionate Impact

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Athlete-tagged posts | 21 (23.3% of total) |
| Total likes from athlete posts | 86,735 (44.8% of all likes) |

23% of their posts generate 45% of all their likes. The athlete program is the engine. Without it, the rest of the program is low-performing gifting noise.

Key athlete accounts: cameronbrink22 (Cameron Brink, WNBA), jpegula (Jessica Pegula, tennis), omahasupernovas (pro volleyball), sundevilwbb/sundevilathletics/asudanceteam/sundevilsoccer (ASU sports).

## Whitespace for KKlue

**Sports lane is taken.** 23% of gorjana posts, 45% of their likes. Don't compete.

**Asian/Korean cultural aesthetic creators = zero gorjana presence.** Not one creator in this dataset has a Korean or Asian cultural identity signal. KKlue's natural lane: Asian-American lifestyle, fashion, beauty micro-influencers. Brand origin is a feature, not baggage.

**The affiliate layer is thin.** Only 17 affiliate posts total. If KKlue builds a ShopMy/Shopify Collabs affiliate program early and recruits aggressively in the fashion/lifestyle micro space, there's room to own that conversion layer in niches gorjana hasn't touched.

**Cleaner disclosure as brand positioning.** 80% of gorjana's program is undisclosed. If KKlue enforces proper disclosure from day one, it signals professionalism to press and retail buyers. They notice.

**Evergreen vs. seasonal.** Gorjana only activates hard in Oct-Feb. An evergreen micro program (6-12 month ambassador relationships with 2 posts/month) would build consistent brand presence vs. their holiday spikes.

---

## Skeptic's Core Point

Gorjana's program only looks impressive because of Cameron Brink. Strip out the top 5 athlete posts and 55% of the remaining posts get fewer than 500 likes. The gifting program is mostly noise. KKlue doesn't need to beat that scale. A tighter program with 20-30 high-quality Asian-American micro-influencers running proper affiliate tracking would likely outperform gorjana's mid-tier on a per-dollar basis.
